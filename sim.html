Unity Robotics Simulator

A simulator for rehearsing autonomous routines and strategy before the real robot exists, so we stop competing for time on one physical bot.

Unity Robot model import Multiplayer Driver practice

Why it exists

On an FRC team, the physical robot is the bottleneck. There is one of it, it is often half disassembled, and everyone — drivers, programmers, strategists — needs time on it. A simulator removes that bottleneck. If the field, the robot, and the controls live in software, people can practice and iterate in parallel, and a bad autonomous routine fails in the sim instead of breaking real hardware.

What I worked on

My focus was making the simulator usable for our team specifically:

Custom robot-model import

Bringing our own robot model into the sim so practice happens with our real geometry and behavior, not a generic stand-in. This makes driver muscle memory and autonomous tuning actually transfer to the physical bot.

Multiplayer testing

Exploring a shared session so a driver and a programmer can be in the same simulated field at once, which is how real practice and strategy work happen — together, not in isolation.
